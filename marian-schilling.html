Be Extraordinary Groups · Week 2

Marian Schilling

#MISCARRIAGE · 6:20

Watch and Listen

Summary

Marian Schilling shares her life experiences. She talks about moving to different places, losing a baby, and finding solace in her husband's words of gratitude for their daughter. Marian emphasizes the importance of loving God, serving Him, and loving one's neighbor. She concludes by expressing her anticipation of being reunited with her late husband and the assurance that nothing can separate us from the love of God.

Appropriate Bible Verses

Psalm 107:1: "Give thanks to the Lord, for he is good; his love endures forever."

Romans 8:38-39: "For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus, our Lord."

Deuteronomy 6:5: "Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your strength."

1 Peter 4:10: "Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God's grace in its various forms."

1 Corinthians 13:13: "And now these three remain: faith, hope, and love. But the greatest of these is love."

Group Discussion

In Marian Schilling's story, we see the importance of faith and gratitude in overcoming challenges. Can you share a personal experience where faith or gratitude played a significant role in helping you navigate a difficult situation or loss?

Marian emphasized the value of loving God and loving our neighbors. How do you practice love in your daily life, whether it's through acts of kindness, compassion, or service to others? Can you share an example of a time when you felt this love in action?

The conversation with Marian Schilling reminds us of the enduring love of God, as mentioned in Romans 8:38-39. How has your faith and the knowledge of God's unwavering love influenced the way you approach life's challenges and setbacks?

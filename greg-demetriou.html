Be Extraordinary Groups · Week 3

Greg Demetriou

#SHOT · 5:37

Watch and Listen

Summary

Greg shares the painful memory of being shot in Grand Central Station during a chase. Despite the traumatic experience, he reflects on how it positively impacted his life. Greg mentions feeling a calmness as he thought he was going to die, apologizing to God and waiting for his last moments. However, he survived and developed post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). This event led Greg to become passionate about helping combat PTSD-related suicides. He also acknowledges the role of faith in his journey, keeping him humble and grateful for the blessings he has. Greg discusses an instance where he regrets not showing forgiveness towards someone he fired, recognizing the need for personal growth and learning from past mistakes.

Overall, Greg's story highlights the transformative power of painful experiences and the importance of faith in finding purpose and growth.

Group Discussion

Question on Finding Purpose in Adversity:

Discussion Question: Greg experienced a traumatic event when he was shot, and he reflects on how it ultimately led to positive changes in his life. Can you share a personal experience where a painful or challenging event later resulted in a positive transformation or a change in your perspective on life?

Relevant Bible Verse: Jeremiah 29:11 (NIV) - "For I know the plans I have for you, declares the Lord, plans for welfare and not for evil, to give you a future and a hope."

Application to Life: Discuss how challenges and adversity can be part of a larger plan for personal growth and a hopeful future. How can the group apply the principle of finding purpose in adversity, as described in this verse, to their own lives?

Question on Forgiveness and Regret:

Discussion Question: Greg mentioned a time when he regrets not showing forgiveness toward someone he had to fire. Forgiveness is a powerful concept in faith. Are there instances in your life where you struggled with forgiveness, either towards yourself or others? How has faith played a role in your ability to forgive or seek forgiveness?

Relevant Bible Verse: Matthew 6:14-15 (NIV) - "For if you forgive other people when they sin against you, your heavenly Father will also forgive you. But if you do not forgive others their sins, your Father will not forgive your sins."

Application to Life: Explore the significance of forgiveness in faith and how it aligns with the teachings in the provided Bible verse. Share personal experiences related to forgiveness and how embracing forgiveness, as both a giver and receiver, can lead to spiritual growth and healing.

Question on Faith and Humility:

Discussion Question: Greg mentioned that faith keeps him humble and thankful for his blessings. How does faith contribute to humility and gratitude in your own life? Can you recall moments when faith helped you maintain a humble attitude in the face of challenges or successes?

Relevant Bible Verse: Philippians 2:3 (NIV) - "Do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit. Rather, in humility, value others above yourselves."

Application to Life: Discuss the role of faith in promoting humility and valuing others above oneself, as emphasized in the provided Bible verse. How can the group apply these principles in daily life to foster a sense of humility, gratitude, and consideration for others?

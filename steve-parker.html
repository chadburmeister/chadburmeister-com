Be Extraordinary Groups · Week 1

Steve Parker

#LIFELESSON · 7:24

Watch and Listen

Summary

Steve Parker reflects on challenging experiences that shaped his life. He shares a childhood memory of living in a roach-infested apartment, which instilled a lasting fear of roaches. He also recounts a lesson in accountability when he was caught taking pens and pencils from a locker room. His father made him return the items to each player individually, teaching him the importance of being responsible for his actions. Steve also discusses his faith journey, expressing his belief in God while acknowledging moments of doubt and questioning. He shares that despite his struggles, he continues to pray every night and seeks calm, wellness, and trust in his faith.

Overall, Steve Parker's experiences highlight the impact of difficult moments on personal growth and the ongoing journey of faith.

Appropriate Bible Verses

Proverbs 3:5-6 (NIV): "Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight."

James 1:2-4 (NIV): "Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds because you know that the testing of your faith produces perseverance. Let perseverance finish its work so that you may be mature and complete, not lacking anything."

Psalm 73:26 (NIV): "My flesh and my heart may fail, but God is the strength of my heart and my portion forever."

Philippians 4:6-7 (NIV): "Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God. And the peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus."

Romans 8:28 (NIV): "And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose." This verse underscores the idea that even in times of doubt and questioning, God's plan is ultimately for the good of those who have faith.

Group Discussion

Steve Parker describes the childhood incident with the roach-infested apartment as a defining memory that still affects him today. Can you think of a childhood memory that has left a lasting impact on you, whether positive or negative, and how has it influenced your life or faith journey?

Steve Parker learned a valuable lesson about accountability when his father made him return stolen pens and pencils to his peers. How can this story relate to the concept of accountability and responsibility in our lives, and how does it align with the principles of faith?

Steve shares his complex relationship with faith, expressing both belief in God and moments of doubt and questioning. Can you relate to this fluctuation in faith and belief in your own life? How do you navigate the moments of uncertainty and continue to seek spiritual growth and trust in your faith?

Be Extraordinary Groups · Week 2

Martin Sawa

#DEATH · 4:53

▶  Watch and Listen

Summary

Chad and Martin Sawa discuss a challenging period in Martin's life - the sudden and unexpected passing of his second wife. Martin describes how this event completely altered the trajectory of his life, as his wife was not only his partner but also his spiritual mentor and moral compass. He shares that dealing with grief was a near-term process that put him on the edge, but he eventually took a self-directed recovery approach and took time off from his career to study and explore the metaphysical realm. Martin emphasizes that the longer-term process of finding meaning and what one believes to be true is an ongoing journey for the rest of one's life.

When asked about faith, Martin reveals that faith has always played a significant role in his life. He was raised Roman Catholic and had a strong religious upbringing. However, he drifted away from his faith during college and afterwards due to growing secularism and the busyness of life. After his wife's passing, he started studying other religions, including Buddhism and Judaism. At the age of almost 60, he attended a personal retreat at a Jesuit Retreat Center, which reignited his practice of faith.

In summary, this transcript highlights Martin Sawa's experience of facing the painful loss of his second wife and the subsequent impact on his life. It explores the near-term process of dealing with grief and the longer-term process of finding meaning and beliefs. Additionally, it touches on Martin's journey with faith, from his Catholic upbringing to his exploration of other religions and his eventual return to practicing his faith.

Group Discussion

Question on Finding Silver Linings in Life's Challenges:

Discussion Question: Martin shared the deeply challenging experience of losing his second wife, which had a profound impact on his life. Can you recall a time when you faced a difficult and unexpected life event? How did you navigate the process of healing and finding a silver lining in that situation?

Relevant Bible Verse: Psalm 34:18 (NIV) - "The Lord is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit."

Application to Life: Reflect on the provided Bible verse and discuss how faith can provide comfort and healing during challenging times. Share personal experiences of overcoming adversity and finding hope, strength, or a silver lining through faith.

Question on Personal Faith Journey:

Discussion Question: Martin's faith journey involved exploring various religions and attending a personal retreat at a Jesuit Center. Have you ever experienced a period in your life where you drifted away from your faith? What inspired or motivated you to rekindle your spiritual journey? If you haven't experienced such a phase, how do you think you would approach it if you did?

Relevant Bible Verse: James 4:8 (NIV) - "Come near to God and he will come near to you."

Application to Life: Discuss the idea of returning to one's faith and seeking a closer relationship with God, as suggested in the provided Bible verse. Share personal insights on the role of faith in your life's journey and how drawing nearer to God has impacted your experiences and beliefs.

Question on the Ongoing Journey of Faith:

Discussion Question: Martin mentioned that the longer-term process after the loss of his wife involves an ongoing journey of finding meaning and discovering what he believes to be true. How do you envision your own journey of faith unfolding over time? What role does the pursuit of meaning and truth play in your faith journey?

Relevant Bible Verse: Proverbs 2:3-5 (NIV) - "Indeed, if you call out for insight and cry aloud for understanding, and if you look for it as for silver and search for it as for hidden treasure, then you will understand the fear of the Lord and find the knowledge of God."

Application to Life: Explore the concept of seeking insight, understanding, and knowledge of God, as highlighted in the provided Bible verse. Discuss the significance of lifelong learning and growth in one's faith journey and how it relates to the pursuit of meaning and truth.

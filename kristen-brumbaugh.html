Be Extraordinary Groups · Week 1

Kristen Brumbaugh

#TAKEN · 5:15

▶  Watch and Listen

Summary

In this emotional conversation, Kristen Brumbaugh opens up about a painful memory from her childhood. At the age of five or six, she was approached by a group of men who tried to abduct her. Although she managed to escape, the experience deeply affected her and made her more cautious and vigilant as a mother. Despite the fear and trauma associated with the memory, Kristen views it as a gift that has made her appreciate her family more and instilled in her a sense of protectiveness towards children. She credits her faith in God for providing comfort and reassurance, knowing that He has a plan for her life and she can trust in His guidance.

The role of Kristen's faith becomes evident throughout the conversation, as she expresses her belief that God has her story laid out and she doesn't have to force things to happen. She emphasizes the importance of trusting, following, and believing in Him, even though it can be challenging to be patient during difficult times. Kristen acknowledges her occasional impatience but attests to the fact that she has witnessed her faith playing out and things coming together in the end.

Appropriate Bible Verses

Proverbs 3:5-6 - "Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight." Kristen's faith in God's plan and guidance aligns with this verse, emphasizing trust and reliance on a higher power.

Psalm 46:1 - "God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble." Kristen's escape from a dangerous situation and her sense of protectiveness towards children can be seen as a testament to God's protection and guidance in her life.

Isaiah 41:10 - "So do not fear, for I am with you; do not be dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you and help you; I will uphold you with my righteous right hand." Kristen's traumatic childhood experience, while frightening, can be viewed through the lens of God's presence and protection.

Romans 8:28 - "And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose." Kristen's journey of being a more cautious and vigilant mother, as well as her commitment to protecting children, reflects her understanding of God's purpose and the potential for good even in challenging experiences.

2 Corinthians 5:7 - "For we live by faith, not by sight." Kristen's reliance on her faith throughout her life's journey, especially during challenging times, is exemplified in this verse. Her belief in God's plan and guidance helps her navigate life with unwavering faith.

Group Discussion

How does Kristen's experience of a traumatic event in her childhood, and her subsequent protective instincts as a mother, demonstrate the concept of God's presence and guidance in our lives, even during challenging times? Can you think of similar instances in your life where you felt God's protection or guidance?

Kristen mentions that she believes God has her life's story laid out and that she doesn't need to force things to happen. How can we apply this concept of trust, follow, and believe in our own lives? Have you ever struggled with impatience during difficult times, and if so, how did you eventually see God's plan come together in those situations?

In the discussion, faith is portrayed as a source of comfort and reassurance, especially in the face of adversity. Can you share a personal story from your life where your faith played a significant role in providing comfort or guidance during challenging circumstances? How did that experience strengthen your relationship with God or impact your journey of faith?

Be Extraordinary Groups · Week 7

Gary VanDyke

#DO SOMETHING · 8:22

▶  Watch and Listen

Summary

Gary VanDyke shares a personal story of facing a devastating divorce and the emotional toll it took on him. However, this experience led him to have a life-changing encounter when he visited poverty-stricken children in Peru. Witnessing their suffering compelled him to take action and make a difference in their lives. With a strong sense of faith, VanDyke started a mission to provide food for orphans, and over the years, they have impacted thousands of lives. He emphasizes the crucial role that faith plays in his life, guiding him away from destructive paths and towards a purposeful journey.

In summary, Gary VanDyke's life took a challenging turn with a heartbreaking divorce, but it ultimately led him to a transformative experience in Peru. His encounter with poverty inspired him to start a mission to feed orphans, driven by his faith. VanDyke's story serves as a testament to the power of turning personal hardships into opportunities for positive change and the importance of faith in finding direction and purpose in life.

Group Discussion

Question on Finding Purpose Through Adversity:

Discussion Question: Gary VanDyke shared that he went through a devastating divorce that had a severe impact on him. However, this difficult experience eventually led him to a life-changing mission. Can you think of a time in your life when adversity or a tough situation ultimately led to a positive change or a new sense of purpose? How did your faith play a role in guiding you through challenging times?

Relevant Bible Verse: Romans 8:28 (NIV) - "And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose."

Application to Life: Discuss the concept of finding purpose and positive outcomes through adversity, as suggested in the provided Bible verse. Share personal stories of how faith and challenging experiences have led to personal growth and new opportunities.

2. Question on Compassion and Action:

Discussion Question: Gary VanDyke's encounter with poverty in Peru led him to take action to help orphaned children. Have you ever been deeply moved by a cause or an issue to the point where you felt compelled to take action? How did faith play a role in motivating you to make a difference?

Relevant Bible Verse: James 2:14-17 (NIV) - "What good is it, my brothers and sisters, if someone claims to have faith but has no deeds? Can such faith save them? Suppose a brother or a sister is without clothes and daily food. If one of you says to them, 'Go in peace; keep warm and well fed,' but does nothing about their physical needs, what good is it? In the same way, faith by itself, if it is not accompanied by action, is dead."

Application to Life: Discuss the relationship between faith and action, as highlighted in the provided Bible verse. Share experiences of how faith has driven you to take meaningful action to address pressing issues in the world.

3. Question on the Role of Faith in Guiding Life Choices:

Discussion Question: Gary VanDyke mentions that faith serves as a guiding force in his life, helping him avoid negative paths and stay on course. How has your faith played a role in guiding your life choices and decisions? Are there moments where you've felt that your faith provided direction and prevented you from making detrimental choices?

Relevant Bible Verse: Proverbs 3:5-6 (NIV) - "Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight."

Application to Life: Explore the idea of trusting in God's guidance and how it can lead to making better life choices, as indicated in the provided Bible verse. Share personal stories of times when your faith played a pivotal role in helping you make decisions that led to positive outcomes.

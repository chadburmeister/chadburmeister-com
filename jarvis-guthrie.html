Be Extraordinary Groups · Week 7

Jarvis Guthrie

#REDEEMED · 41 min

Watch and Listen

Summary

Jarvis Guthrie, a former inmate turned prison minister, shares his transformative journey from incarceration to redemption. Facing a two-year prison sentence and three years of house arrest after mistakenly breaking into a police officer's house while intoxicated, Jarvis found solace and inspiration in his encounter with his pastor while in jail. This encounter led him to turn to God and dedicate his life to helping others in similar situations. Now serving as a prison minister, Jarvis provides spiritual guidance and support to inmates, including those on death row. His faith in God and the support of his family have helped him find hope and purpose in his life, inspiring and transforming the lives of others.

Throughout the conversation, Jarvis reflects on the events that led him to prison, expressing remorse for his actions and acknowledging the grace and mercy that spared his life during the encounter with the police officer. He discusses the challenges faced by ex-prisoners upon release, such as limited support systems and discrimination in various aspects of life. Jarvis advocates for reentry programs and emphasizes the importance of faith and finding one's identity in Christ. Despite his own difficulties transitioning back into society, Jarvis remains resilient and determined, pursuing higher education and finding purpose in ministering to others. He envisions a future where he can focus on his farm and prison ministry full-time, relying on God's provision and grace to sustain him in his mission to help others.

Group Discussion

1. Question on Overcoming Adversity:

Discussion Question: Jarvis faced a life-altering event and a prison sentence after an unfortunate incident. Can you think of a time in your life when you encountered a significant setback or challenge that you had to overcome? How did you navigate it, and what lessons did you learn from that experience?

Relevant Bible Verse: Romans 8:28 (NIV) - "And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose."

Application to Life: Discuss the idea that even in difficult times, God may have a purpose and plan for us, as indicated in the provided Bible verse. Share personal stories of how you've managed adversity and how it has contributed to your growth and faith.

2. Question on Transforming Identity and Purpose:

Discussion Question: Jarvis transitioned from a challenging past to becoming a pastor and working with prison ministry. How did he transform his identity from the mistakes of his past to the impactful work he does today? Have you ever experienced a transformation in your identity or purpose? What influenced that change?

Relevant Bible Verse: 2 Corinthians 5:17 (NIV) - "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!"

Application to Life: Explore the concept of transformation and renewal in Christ, as suggested in the provided Bible verse. Share experiences of personal growth, renewal, and how faith played a role in changing your identity or purpose.

3. Question on Finding Hope and Purpose Through Isolation:

Discussion Question: Jarvis mentioned being isolated in prison as an opportunity for personal growth and spiritual development. How has isolation or challenging circumstances in your life shaped your faith or character? What lessons can be learned from times of solitude or adversity?

Relevant Bible Verse: Psalms 46:10 (NIV) - "Be still, and know that I am God; I will be exalted among the nations, I will be exalted in the earth."

Application to Life: Discuss the significance of finding solace and spiritual growth during times of isolation or difficulty, as suggested in the provided Bible verse. Share experiences of how solitude or challenging moments have deepened your faith and understanding of God.
